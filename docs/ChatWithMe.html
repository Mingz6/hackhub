Talking Points
Slide 1 of 14

Open strong. ChatWithMe is a browser plugin we built to help absolute beginners navigate AI platforms without getting lost or frustrated.

Our team of 4 — Min and Ming on engineering, Yiyin on design, Andrew on product. We had 24 hours. Here’s what we built.

The problem is massive and real. Everyone is talking about AI. Everyone wants to use it. But 80% of people who encounter a new AI platform quit in under 60 seconds.

Not because they’re not smart — because the interface is designed for engineers, not humans.

This is what they see. API endpoints. Environment variables. Model parameters. Token limits. System prompts in English.

For someone who just wants to “talk to an AI” — this is a wall. And most people don’t climb it. They close the tab.

This is personal. Every one of us has watched someone close to them — a parent, a colleague, a friend — struggle with AI and give up.

We believe in the AI era, nobody should get left behind. The platform should come to the user, not the other way around.

The numbers are brutal. 79% of annual AI subscribers churn within a year. Compare: 21% AI retention versus 31% for traditional apps. Monthly is even worse — 6% vs 10%.

This is not a product problem. It’s an onboarding crisis. And it’s costing SaaS companies billions.

The cliff is steep and fast. 71% attrition in the first month — with the steepest drop happening in the very first session.

70% of users who encounter a complex interface close it in under 60 seconds. Before they’ve even tried the product. That’s a massive loss of potential.

It’s not complexity itself — it’s unfamiliar complexity. English terms when your mother tongue is Chinese. API keys with no explanation. URLs that look like server configs.

When cognitive load exceeds the critical threshold, curiosity dies instantly. “Don’t make me think” — Steve Krug said it about web design. It applies 10x to AI onboarding.

Pause here for effect. This is the pivot moment in the presentation.

We asked ourselves: what if the platform could guide you? What if instead of documentation, you had a co-pilot that lives right on the page? That’s ChatWithMe.

First principle: one box, plain language. No menus, no settings, no documentation links. A single floating sidebar with one input.

Users type what they want in plain language — Chinese or English. “Where do I click to start?” “Where does the API key go?” The plugin handles everything from there.

These are real questions from user testing. Not edge cases — these are the first things every beginner asks.

ChatWithMe doesn’t answer with text walls. It shows you exactly what to click, live on the page. Zero reading required. Just watch and follow.

The soul feature: Spotlight Mode. When the user asks a question, the page dims. A glowing ring appears around the exact button they need. A bouncing arrow points at it.

Zero cognitive load. The user doesn’t need to read anything or make a decision. Just follow the light.

We went further than just pointing. ChatWithMe tracks actual clicks. Step 2 never activates until step 1 is done — it enforces the correct operational sequence.

If the user clicks the wrong thing, no cold error message. Just a gentle, friendly redirect. Like a patient teacher who never gets frustrated.

The business case is clear. For SaaS companies hemorrhaging 79% of their users, ChatWithMe recovers 15–25% of those lost users by eliminating the “first minute barrier.”

It accelerates time-to-value — the critical moment users go “aha, I get it now.” And it’s not just a plugin — it’s visual guidance middleware embeddable in any complex web app.

That’s ChatWithMe. Simple concept, powerful impact. We believe this solves a real problem — not just for CLōD, but for every AI platform that wants to grow beyond early adopters.

Open to questions. Thank you.
